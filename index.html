Primary educational stage in Egypt

The development of the primary education stage The first stage of formal education in Egypt, which extends for a period of six years, starting from the first grade of primary school and ending with the sixth grade of primary school.
Objectives of the primary stage
Primary education mission to accomplish the following tasks:

  • Developing the student’s ability to read, count, and basic skills.
  • Develop thinking skills in critical solutions and problem analysis.
  • Developing communication and interaction skills with others.
  • Developing the skills of loving the homeland and belonging to it.

    • Preparatory education stage in Egypt

    The preparatory education stage is the second stage of formal education in Egypt, and it extends for three years, starting from the first year of middle school and ending with the third year of middle school.Objectives of the preparatory stage The preparatory education stage aims to achieve the following goals:

  • Deepen students' abilities in reading, writing, numeracy and other basic skills.
  • Developing students’ skills in critical and analytical thinking and problem solving.
  • Developing students’ skills in communicating and interacting with others.
  • Preparing students to enter secondary education.

    • Secondary education stage in Egypt

    The secondary education stage is the third stage of formal education in Egypt, and it extends for three years, starting from the first year of secondary school and ending with the third year of secondary school.
    Objectives of the secondary education stage The secondary education stage aims to achieve the following goals:

  • Deepening basic knowledge and skills in academic subjects.
  • Developing critical and analytical thinking and problem-solving skills.
  • Developing students’ skills in communicating and interacting with others.
  • Preparing students to enter higher education or the labor market.



    • Schools in Egypt suffer from a variety of problems

    Student overcrowding: The problem of student overcrowding is one of the most important problems facing education in Egypt. There are on average 40 students per classroom, which makes it difficult for teachers to provide quality education to students.
    Weak efficiency of teachers: The education sector in Egypt suffers from weak efficiency of teachers, as teachers do not receive adequate training, and there is no effective system for evaluating teachers’ performance.
    Inadequate financial resources: Schools in Egypt suffer from insufficient financial resources, as there are insufficient budgets to provide an appropriate educational environment for students.
    Poor quality of the curricula: The curricula in Egypt suffer from poor quality, as they do not reflect the requirements of the labor market, and do not take into account individual differences among students.
    School dropout: Education in Egypt suffers from the problem of school dropout, as many students leave school before completing their primary or preparatory education.
    Low level of academic achievement: Student overcrowding and poor teacher efficiency lead to a low level of academic achievement for students.
    Weak critical and analytical thinking skills: Students are unable to develop critical and analytical thinking skills due to poor quality of the curriculum.
    High unemployment rates: School dropout leads to high unemployment rates among young people.
