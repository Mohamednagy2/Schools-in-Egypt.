Evaluation of schools in Egypt

Schools in Egypt can be evaluated through a set of criteria, including:

  • Level of academic achievement: The level of academic achievement of students is measured through the results of national and international tests.
  • Teachers’ competence: Teachers’ competence is measured through indicators, such as academic qualifications and professional experience.
  • Quality of the curriculum: The quality of the curriculum is measured by its suitability to the needs of students and the requirements of the labor market.
  • Learning environment: The learning environment is measured by the extent to which it provides appropriate educational facilities and means for students.
  • Community participation: Community participation is measured by the extent to which the community contributes to supporting the educational process.
  • Low level of academic achievement: The results of national and international tests indicate that the level of academic achievement of Egyptian students is low compared to other countries.
  • Weak efficiency of teachers: The education sector in Egypt suffers from weak efficiency of teachers, as teachers do not receive adequate training, and there is no effective system for evaluating teachers’ performance.
  • Poor quality of the curricula: The curricula in Egypt suffer from poor quality, as they do not reflect the requirements of the labor market, and do not take into account individual differences among students.
  • Inadequate financial resources: Schools in Egypt suffer from insufficient financial resources, as there are insufficient budgets to provide an appropriate educational environment for students.
  • Low employment opportunities: Students are unable to obtain good job opportunities due to low level of academic achievement.
  • High unemployment rates: Poor quality of education leads to high unemployment rates among youth.
  • Poor social development: Students are unable to contribute to social development due to weak critical and analytical thinking skills.
  • gyptian government is trying to address these problems through a set of measures, including:
  • Expanding the education base: The government is working to expand the education base by establishing new schools and providing educational opportunities for students from deprived areas.
  • Improving the quality of education: The government works to improve the quality of education by developing curricula, training teachers, and providing the necessary material resources to schools.
  • Combating school dropout: The government works to combat school dropout by providing material and moral support to students and their families.
